Qutub-Minar one of the tallest minarets in the world

Kalka Das Marg, Mehrauli, New Delhi - 110030

The Great History of Qutub-Minar:-

  • Built in the early 13th century a few kilometres south of Delhi, the red sandstone tower of Qutb Minar is 72.5 m high, tapering from 2.75 m in diameter at its peak to 14.32 m at its base, and alternating angular and rounded flutings.
  • Muhammad Ghori (founder of Muslim rule in India) had triumphed over the Rajput dynasty at bringing bringing Islamic rule to India. He defeated Delhi’s last Hindu ruler.
  • His general Qutb-ud-Din Aibak became the first Islamic ruler of north India, and the construction of the first Islamic building (Qutub Minar) began.
  • This building was a commemoration of a great victory

Why to visit?:-

  • A UNESCO World Heritage Site, and the tallest building in the world made of bricks
  • Qutub Minar is a minaret and "victory tower" that forms part of the Qutb complex
  • Built-in red sandstone, Qutub Minar is a real masterpiece of Mughal Islamic craftsmanship .It's a great opportunity to immerse oneself in Indian culture.
  • It is one of the most visited tourist spots in the city, mostly built between 1199 and 1220.

Best Time to Visit:-

  • The best time to visit Qutub Minar generally is in the evening as the monument looks stunning in the golden hue of the setting sun .
  • if you want to witness the ultimate charm of the minaret, you have got to see it all lit-up at night.
  • If you want to make your Qutub Minar visit all the more memorable, it is advised that you plan your Delhi tour in the month of October to February .

Nearby-places you must visit..

India Gate
  • India Gate is a war memorial dedicated to honor the soldiers of the Undivided Indian Army who died during World War I between 1914 and 1921.
  • A National War Memorial to honor all armed forces members killed after 1947 is under construction at the ‘C’ Hexagon of India Gate.
    • Red-Fort
  • The Red Fort, which is also known as the Lal Qila, was constructed by one of the most famous Mughal emperors, Shah Jahan.
  • It took over the honor of royal residence from the famous Agra Fort when Shah Jahan decided to shift his capital from Agra to Delhi.
    • Akshardham temple
  • The Akshardham Temple, also known as Swaminarayan Akshardham is dedicated to Lord Swaminarayan
  • Akshardham complex is home to India's largest step well which is a host to the mesmerising water show each evening.
    • Lotus Temple
  • Lotus Temple is an architectural marvel that is famous for its notable lotus structure.
  • Based on the Baha'i faith of worship, Lotus Temple in New Delhi believes inequality among all religions. Speaking out of travelers’ experience.
    • Humayun's Tomb
  • Humayun’s Tomb, Delhi is the first of the grand dynastic mausoleums that were to become synonyms of Mughal architecture
  • Humayun’s garden-tomb is also called the ‘dormitory of the Mughals’ as in the cells are buried over 150 Mughal family members.
