Ooty

Udhagamandalam (Ooty), Tamil Nadu, popularly referred as the 'Queen of Hill Stations.

History

  • The Nilgiris was part of the Vijayanagar Kingdom from 1336 to 1565. After its fall in 1565 the rulers of Mysore gained control over the Nilgiris. Later it came under the rule of Hyder Ali and Tipu Sultan (1760 to 1799). The Nilgiris was ceded to the East India Company in 1799 by a Treaty of Srirangapattanam.

  • The most important thing that makes Ooty always a best place to visit is the climate, Eventhough the season is believed to be from April to June. Originally occupied by the Badaga and Toda people, the area came under the rule of the East India Company at the end of the 18th century.

  • It was first discovered during the British rule, by the collector of coimbatore called john Sullivan in the year 1817. The name udagamandalam is derived from the word “ottakkalmandu” which means “A village of a single stone” in Tamil language. But, afterwards people used to call this place as ooty in short form.

  • Ooty was the Madras Presidency summer capital and the headquarters of the Nilgiris District. It is popularly called the "Queen of Hill Stations".

Why to visit Ooty ?

  • A UNESCO World Heritage, a ride on the Nilgiri Mountain Railways is an experience of a lifetime. Other points of tourist interest include the Avalanche Lake, Upper Bhavani Lake and the Emerald Lake. Boating at the famous Ooty Lake also qualifies as one of the most exciting things to do in the hill station.

  • This region is entirely a group of mountains which are Natural sky scrappers. The Mountains are so high, such that we can see the clouds crossing the way when we Travel.

  • Another important experience which every Traveler gets when they come here is the Dangerous, but thrilling Hair-pin Bends. Our mind would record an excellent scenic Beauty which is immortal when we Travel through this way. Not only the sceneries But also the minute frame work of Tamilnadu Roadways and valuable support provided by Tourism development corporation of Tamilnadu is seen.

  • Tea and Chocolate lovers, you cannot miss this place. The tea that grows here has its own aromatic flavour. The bakeries around here make “Varkeys” that you can have along with your tea and thereby doubling the taste of your tea.

Best time to visit Ooty

  • Ooty in Summer
    Beat the heat of summer with the pleasant weather of Ooty from March to June; this is the best time to visit Ooty. Go sightseeing at Avalanche Lake, Botanical Garden, and Ooty Lake. The temperature of this place ranges between 20°C-25°C throughout the summer season. Choose a clear night and go star-gazing. Summers are the best time to visit Ooty for camping and star-gazing.

  • Ooty in Monsoon
    If you love rains then Monsoon is the best time to visit Ooty. This paradise receives heavy rainfall from July to September. After the rain, the sky looks totally different, as if bathed in a new light. Nature lovers can embrace the beauty of Dodabetta, Glenmorgan, and Pykara Lake. You can enjoy some peace and calm in these amazing locations.

  • Ooty in Winters
    The winter months last from October to February and any month in that period is the best month to visit Ooty for the chilly weather. The overall temperature of the day is cool and perfect for sightseeing. Enjoy a ride in the toy train or visit Mudumalai Wildlife Sanctuary on a bright sunny day.

  • At night, the temperature can go below 5°C. Don't forget to carry warm clothes with you. Though this may not be the best climate to visit Ooty, in the evening, you can make plans to go out early in the evening and doing some less intense activities. You could take your family out for shopping and hot snacks.



Foods you wouldn't wanna miss in Ooty

Idli and Chutney

It's a traditional savoury breakfast from South India. Idlis are soft pillowy moon-like cakes made of steamed rice and lentil. Millet and vegetables can be added to this simple recipe. It's a healthy probiotic dish!

Dosa

It's a traditional savoury breakfast from South India. Idlis are soft pillowy moon-like cakes made of steamed rice and lentil. Millet and vegetables can be added to this simple recipe. It's a healthy probiotic dish!

Uttapam

Unlike a typical dosa, which is crisp, an uttapam is thicker, with toppings. The name is derived from the Tamil words appam and utthia or uttria, meaning "poured appam", because appam is cooked in a round-bottom pan, whereas utthia-appam is cooked on a flat skillet.

Chicken Chettinad

Chicken Chettinad is a classic south Indian delicacy that is laced in peppery authentic gravy that is prepared from coconut milk. This southern-style chicken dish contains plenty of spices and herbs and contains well-balanced flavours of spiciness and silkiness of coconut.



Places to visit at least once when you are in Ooty...

Avalanche Lake

15 km from city center

The Avalanche Lake in the Nilgiris District of Tamil Nadu is a famous tourist spot. Especially renowned among nature enthusiasts and photographers, the lake is set amidst a captivating landscape which is filled with lovely flowers.

Ooty Rose Garden

2 km from city center

Rose garden lies in the heart and soul of Ooty.The construction stays to be magnificent and there are small tunnels made wherein the roses are planted.

Nilgiri Mountain Railway

1 km from city center

Nilgiri Mountain Railway, also known as Toy Train in Ooty, is one of the key attractions of the popular hill station Ooty. Such is the train's popularity and history that it was declared as a World Heritage Site by UNESCO in 2005.

Pykara Waterfall

12 km from city center

Pykara Lake has everything that a person on vacation can ask for, right from ambience and comfort to scenic beauty. Nature lovers, photographers and adventure seekers should definitely not miss out on this unique and fun-filled place.
