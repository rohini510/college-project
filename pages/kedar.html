Rudraprayag district of Uttarakhand,India 246445

The Great History of Kedarnath:-

  • According to Hindu legends, the temple was initially built by the Pandavas, and is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas, the holiest Hindu shrines of Shiva.
  • The Pandavas were supposed to have pleased Shiva by doing penance in Kedarnath.
  • The temple was originally built in 8th century A.D. by Jagad Guru Adi Shankaracharya and stands adjacent to site of an even earlier temple built by the Pandavas
  • The historical name of this region is "Kedar Khand".

Why to visit?:-

  • Kedarnath is one of the twelve Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva.
  • Kedarnath Yatra is a well-known pilgrimage destination for Chota Char Dham and home to the Kedarnath Temple.
    • .
  • It's a great opportunity to immerse oneself in Indian culture.
  • Kedarnath, a green valley with meandering streams and snow-capped peaks, is part of the Garhwal Himalayan Range.

Best Time to Visit:-

  • The best time to visit Kedarnath is the summer season, from April to June.
  • You can also plan Kedarnath Yatra during the pre-winter months, September-October.
  • This sacred Dham or Temple remains closed from November-March (winter) due to extreme cold and heavy snowfall.
  • Avoid visits during Winter.

Don't give a miss to this food when in Kedarnath!

Dubuk

It is a kind of Dal gravy, prepared with the help of either Arhar, Gahat or other lentil of one’s choice. It is one of the most traditional dishes of the Kumaoni people which they usually make on events and some special occasions
Phaana

It is kind a kind of pulses which is first left to soak for 8 hours and then later it is grinded and make a thick batter
Kode ki Roti

This roti is mixed with Daal and then eaten with joy and it is also an incredibly rich in iron and fibre.
Aloo ke Gutke Kumaoni

This is a famos Kumaoni dish with an extremely easy method of preparation, yet considered to be culinary gem of the region
Chainsoo

This is a protein rich dish prepared by using black lentils.




Places you can also visit.....

Bhadrinath
  • Badrinath is a town and nagar panchayat in Chamoli district in the state of Uttarakhand, India
  • Badrinath was re-established as a major pilgrimage site by Adi Shankara in the 8th century
  • The Badrinath temple is the main attraction in the town.
Haridwar
  • Haridwar is regarded as a holy place for Hindus, hosting important religious events and serving as a gateway to several prominent places of worship.
  • The city is situated on the right bank of the Ganges river, at the foothills of the Shivalik ranges.
Gaurikund
  • The hot water spring of Gaurikund is one of the holiest sites for Hindus, who come to take a dip in it.
  • It is believed that taking a dip in the holy water of the pond will make a person pure.
  • Relaxing and peaceful atmosphere.
SonPrayag
  • Sonprayag is a place of religious significance located enroute to Kedarnath Dham.
  • Prayag means confluence and Sonprayag lies at the confluence of two holy rivers Basuki and Mandakini.
Rishikesh
  • Rishikesh is a vegetarian-only and alcohol-free city..
  • Rishikesh is a city in India’s northern state of Uttarakhand, in the Himalayan foothills beside the Ganges River
