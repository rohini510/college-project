Taj-Mahal A Monument to Love...

Dharmapuri, Forest Colony, Tajganj, Agra, Uttar Pradesh 282001

The Great History of Taj-Mahal:-

  • The Taj Mahal, a stunning white marble mausoleum in Agra, India, is a symbol of love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture.
  • Shah Jahan, the fifth Mughal emperor, built the Taj Mahal in memory of his beloved queen, Mumtaz Mahal, who died during childbirth.
  • Mumtaz's dying wish led Shah Jahan to commission the construction of the Taj Mahal in 1632, creating a unique blend of Persian, Islamic, and Indian architectural styles.
  • In essence, the Taj Mahal is not just a monument but a profound symbol of love, art, and history.

Why to visit?:-

  • The Taj Mahal is a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a symbol of India's rich cultural heritage.
  • Stunning Photography: The Taj Mahal offers incredible photo opportunities.
  • It's a great opportunity to immerse oneself in Indian culture.
  • The surrounding gardens and the reflection pool add to the visual charm.

Best Time to Visit:-

  • The best time to visit Agra is in the winter season between November to March.
  • This weather is perfect for sightseeing.
  • Another good time to visit Agra is between August and October to witness the Taj Mahal at its best.
  • Avoid visits during Summers.

Mehtab Bagh
  • Mehtab Bagh is just 300 meters from Taj mahal on the opposite bank of river Yamuna.
  • This vantage point offers an wonderful and stunning view of the Taj Mahal, especially during sunset.
  • It is a perfect spot for photographers.
  • Relaxing and peaceful atmosphere.
Red Fort
  • Red fort is 2.5 km away from the Taj Mahal
  • It is an iconic symbol of India's rich history and heritage.
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site
  • Inside the fort, there is a covered bazaar known as Chhatta Chowk,where merchants and artisans sold their wares.
Jama Masjid
  • The Jama Masjid is one of the Largest Mosque built by the Mughals in India.
  • The mosque is decorated with intricate carvings and calligraphy.
  • The area surrounding the Jama Masjid is known for its street food.
  • Food enthusiasts must visit it.
Ram Bagh
  • The garden was initially named "Aram Bagh," which means "Garden of Relaxation."
  • The garden is a peaceful place for a leisurely stroll or a picnic.
  • It is Agra’s oldest garden which was established in 1528
  • The garden featured lush greenery, fountains, and fragrant blooms.
Taj Museum
  • This museum is located inside the Taj Mahal complex, on the western side to the entrance of Taj Mahal.
  • Here you can find paintings and photographs of Taj Mahal during different decades.
  • The museum exhibits a collection of Mughal artifacts, including coins, jewelry, clothing, and weapons from the Mughal era.
